RF v Secretary of State for Work and Pensions is a landmark case in disability and human rights law. It reaffirmed equality principles and challenged government policies that discriminated against people with mental health conditions.

Facts

In 2017, new PIP regulations excluded those whose mobility issues stemmed from psychological distress. RF, granted anonymity, challenged the rule, arguing it unfairly discriminated against disabled individuals and sought to have it overturned.

Arguments

The Secretary of State argued the change was a necessary welfare reform expected to save £1 billion annually. RF contended it was discriminatory, breached human rights law, and exceeded the Secretary’s legal authority.

Judgement

The High Court ruled in favor of RF, finding the regulations unlawfully discriminatory toward disabled people. The court held that financial savings could not justify such unequal treatment, and the regulations were quashed.

Impact

The ruling was widely celebrated by disability rights advocates. Organizations like Inclusion London and DPAC praised it as a landmark victory, strengthening protections against government discrimination toward people with disabilities.

Quick Facts

Year: 2017

Court: High Courts

Legal Area: Human Rights (Discrimination)

Key Principle: Disability Discrimination

Outcome: Found in favor of RF; PIP regulations found discriminatory
