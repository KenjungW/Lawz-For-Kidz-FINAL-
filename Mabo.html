Mabo v Queensland (No 2) is a landmark decision in the High Court of Australia. It is notable for being the first case in Australia to recognize pre-colonial land interests of Indigenous Australians.

Facts

The Murray Islands off the coast of Australia were inhabitant by the Meriam people and the land is owned by the eldest son. However, the British made it so Australia was terra nullius, where the land is owned by no one. Therefore, Eddie Mabo and a few other plantiffs bought the case to court.

Arguments

Mabo argued that the Meriam people have occupied the islands for generations, that the court recognize the existence of Native titles, and deem terra nullius invalid. The State of Queensland argued that upon colonization, the Crown became the affective owner of all land.

Judgement

The court ruled in favor of Mabo, deciding that native titles existed under Australian common law for indigenous people. They also overturned the doctrine of terra nullius

Impact

Former prime minister Paul Keating praised the decision, saying it establishes a "fundamental truth". However, the decision was condemned by the government of Western Australia and many pastoralist and mining groups. Mabo Day became an official holiday, celebrated on the 3rd of June, when the ruling was decided.

Quick Facts

Year: 1992

Court: High Court of Australia

Legal Area: Property Law

Key Principle: Indigenous land rights

Outcome: Mabo won, overturned terra nullius.
